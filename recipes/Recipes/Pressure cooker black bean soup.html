Pressure cooker black bean soup

Dinner, Gluten Free, InstantPot, large batch, Soup

Ingredients

  • Soup:
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped fine (3 cups)
  • 1 large carrot, chopped fine (1/2 cup)
  • 3 ribs celery, chopped fine (1 cup)
  • 6-8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon minced chipotle chiles in adobo
  • 2 teaspoons adobo sauce
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1-2 cups water
  • 1 pound dried black beans
  • 1-2 lbs smoked ham hocks or ham shanks
  • 2 bay leaves

  • Finish:
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice

  • Toppings:
  • lime wedges
  • cilantro
  • red onion
  • avocado
  • sour cream

Directions

Heat oil in pressure cooker's pot. Add onions, carrot, celery, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Stir occasionally, until vegetables are soft and lightly browned, about 12-15 minutes.

Add garlic and cumin. Cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant, about 3 minutes

Add chipotles, adobo, chicken broth, water, and beans. Stir to combine and scrape frond on bottom of pot.

Add ham and bay leaves.

Cook at high pressure for 30 minutes.

Let pressure release naturally for 10 minutes after cooking, and then manually release the pressure.

Remove the ham from the pot and place on a cutting board. Remove 1 1/2 cups of beans and 2 cups of liquid and blend until smooth. Add blended beans and liquid back to the pot.

Cut the meat off the ham, discarding any skin or gristle. Chop meat into small pieces.

Mix equal parts cornstarch and water in a small bowl, then add to the pot. Bring the pot to boiling and stir, adding more cornstarch or boiling longer until desired soup thickness is reached. Take pot off of heat.

Add chopped meat and lime juice. Ladle soup into bowls and serve with toppings.
