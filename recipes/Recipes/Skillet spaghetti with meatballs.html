Skillet spaghetti with meatballs

Uncategorized

Ingredients

  • Meatballs:
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 4 ounces ground prok
  • 1 ounce grated Parmesan cheese (1/2 cup)
  • 1/4 cup instant potato flakes
  • 3 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 12 ounces 85% lean ground beef
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

  • Sauce and pasta:
  • 3 14.5-ounce cans whole peeled tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • salt
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 12 ounces spaghetti
  • black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh basil leaves
  • Parmesan cheese for serving

Directions

Meatballs:

Whisk together buttermilk and egg yolk in large bowl. Add pork, Parmesan, potato flakes, parsley, garlic, salt, and pepper, and knead with hands until mixture is thoroughly combined. Add beef and continue to knead until uniform. Gently form mixture into 1 1/2 inch round meatballs (about 12 meatballs)

Heat oil in 12-inch non-stick skillet over medium heat until just smoking. Brown meatballs on all sides, about 10 minutes. Transfer meatballs to rimmed baking sheet and bake at 350 F until cooked through, 8-10 minutes.

Sauce and pasta:

Pulse tomatoes and their juice in a food processor until coarsely ground and no large pieces remain, about 12 pulses.

Add 2 tablespoons of oil to non-stick skillet and heat over medium heat until shimmering. Add the onion and 1/2 teaspoon salt and cook, stirring often, until softened, 5 to 7 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Stir in the tomatoes and sugar. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes no longer taste raw, about 10 minutes.

Stir in the water, then add the pasta. Cover, increase the heat to medium-high, and cook, stirring often and adjusting the heat to maintain vigorous simmer, until pasta is tender, 12 to 15 minutes.

Stir in remaining 1 tablespoon of oil and season with salt and pepper. Add meatballs, coat them with sauce, and let them warm through, about 1 minute. Sprinkle with basil and serve.
