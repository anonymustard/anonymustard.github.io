Charcoal grilled pork chops

Dinner, grill, Meat

Prep 60 minutes Cook 20 minutes Makes 4

Ingredients

  • 4 9-12 oz bone-in thick cut pork loin chops
  • 4 tablespoons pork spice rub

  • Brine:
  • 100 grams kosher salt or granulated salt
  • 75 grams granulated sugar
  • 3 quarts water

  • Spice rub:
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar

Directions

1. If brining, dissolve salt and sugar in water to a large zipper-lock bag. Add chops and squeeze out as much air as possible, and seal. Let porks brine in the refrigerator for 1 hour, turning bag once.

2. Ignite large chimney starter filled with hardwood charcoal (about 2 1/2 pounds) and burn until charcoal is covered with layer of fine gray ash. Build a two-level fire by stacking most of coals on one side of grill and arranging remaining coals in single layer on other side. Set cooking grate in place, cover grill with lid, and let grate heat up, about 5 minutes. Use wire brush to scrape cooking grate clean.

3. Cook chops, uncovered, over hotter part of grill until browned on each side, 2 1/2 to 3 minutes per side. Move chops to cooler part of grill and cover with disposable aluminum roasting pan. Continue grilling, turning once, until instant-read thermometer inserted through side of chop and away from bone registers 135 degrees, 7 to 9 minutes longer. Transfer chops to platter; cover with foil pan, and let rest 5 minutes. Internal temperature should rise to 145 degrees. Serve immediately.
